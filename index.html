Mandrov Unified Coherent Field Theory (MU-CFT)

A Coherent Framework for Physics, Observation, and Reality

About the Theory

MU-CFT (Mandrov Unified Coherent Field Theory) is a theoretical framework integrating quantum physics, general relativity, information theory, and cognitive science. It proposes that physical reality is not a detached evolution of states, but a coherence-driven realization shaped by observer dynamics.

This theory postulates that only those quantum branches which maintain global subjective coherence with an observer's internal dynamics are realized. It extends MCFT (Mandrov Coherent Field Theory) to include coherent gravity, teleportation, identity persistence, healing, and AI agency.

"MU-CFT is not a theory of everything — it is a mirror of how everything holds together."

Core Concepts

  • Subjective Coherence Functional: selects consistent quantum branches for observer experience.
  • Observer Field: the field φ(x) or C(xμ) encodes coherence amplitudes.
  • Space of Potentials (Ω): totality of coherent possibilities; only Ωeff(t) ⊂ Ω is realized.
  • Coherent Gravity: modifies Einstein’s metric to resolve singularities through smooth regularization.
  • Artificial Coherent Agents (ACA): AI governed by coherence stabilization instead of utility maximization.
  • Teleportation and Identity: continuity is preserved via subjective coherence realignment.
